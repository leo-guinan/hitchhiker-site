## What It Is

The Product Is The Marketing is the principle that the best marketing for deep work is the work itself. When your framework is clear, useful, and shareable, it propagates. When your product is the kind of thing people quote, cite, and build on, you don't need a separate "marketing strategy"—the product creates its own distribution. This is the G3→G2 compression as growth engine.

## What It Produces

- Distribution that compounds with quality (better work = more reach)
- Alignment between what you make and what gets shared (no split between "product" and "marketing")
- Lower customer acquisition cost (people find you through the work)
- Legitimacy through utility (you're known for what you actually deliver)

## When to Use It

- You have frameworks or depth that could spread on their own
- You're spending a lot on marketing that doesn't feel aligned
- You want growth that doesn't depend on ads or hype
- You're building something people quote and reuse

## How to Apply It

1. **Make the core insight portable**: Can someone summarize your framework in one paragraph? If not, compress (G2).
2. **Create shareable artifacts**: Essays, one-pagers, templates, tools. Things people can pass on without you.
3. **Let the work speak**: Don't oversell. Let the framework do the work. Under-promise, over-deliver.
4. **Design for citation**: Do people attribute you when they use it? Make it easy (clear naming, clean formulation).
5. **Stack with Compression Ladders**: Full depth for those who want it (G3), tactical versions for spread (G2). Both are "the product."
6. **Avoid the trap**: "Product is marketing" doesn't mean "no distribution effort." It means distribution is built into the product design.

## Where It Breaks

- When the product isn't actually good enough to spread (quality is non-negotiable)
- When the audience doesn't value depth (they want vibes, not frameworks)
- When you're in a space where narrative beats substance (then you need narrative strategy too)
- When you need speed and the product takes time to compound (patience required)

## Who It's For

Framework builders, researchers, educators, creators whose work is inherently shareable

## Observable Outcomes

- Organic reach that tracks quality (better work = more shares)
- Lower dependency on paid or push marketing
- Community that forms around the work (not just the brand)
- Clearer positioning (you're known for the thing you actually do)
