## What It Is

Recognition-Based Pricing is the practice of pricing your work in alignment with what you're actually selling in the attention/legitimacy economy. You're not just selling time or deliverablesâ€”you're selling recognition (being known for something), access (relationship, network), and narrative (story, identity). Price for the value that flows through those channels.

## What It Produces

- Pricing that matches value (not just hours)
- Clarity on what buyers are actually paying for (insight, access, status, narrative)
- Multiple price points for different value layers (product vs. service vs. relationship)
- Sustainable revenue from depth work (you're not undercharging for pattern recognition)

## When to Use It

- Pricing consulting, coaching, or advisory work
- Deciding what to put behind a paywall vs. give away
- Building tiers (free content, paid product, premium access)
- Understanding why some people charge 10x for similar-looking work

## How to Apply It

1. **Identify what you're selling**: Time? Outcome? Access? Recognition? Narrative? (Often it's a mix; be explicit.)
2. **Map the buyer's value**: What are they actually buying? (Better decisions, legitimacy by association, access to you, story to tell.)
3. **Price for the real value**: If they're buying access to your network, price for that. If they're buying the framework, price for that. Don't price everything as "hours."
4. **Use tiers**: Free (G2 fragments, spread), Paid product (structured framework, depth), Paid service (coaching, access, implementation). Each tier captures different value.
5. **Connect to Compression Ladders**: Different compressions = different products = different price points. One body of work, multiple revenue streams.
6. **Avoid undercharging for recognition**: If your name or association is part of the value, price it. Pattern recognition is expensive to develop; charge accordingly.

## Where It Breaks

- When you overprice for your actual legitimacy (market won't pay)
- When you underprice because you're selling "time" (you're leaving value on the table)
- When the buyer doesn't understand what they're paying for (alignment failure)
- When you're in a market that only pays for deliverables (recognition has no price there)

## Who It's For

Consultants, coaches, creators, anyone monetizing frameworks or expertise

## Observable Outcomes

- Revenue that reflects the full value you provide
- Clearer value proposition (you know what you're selling)
- Tiers that make sense (free/paid/premium aligned with value layers)
- Better intuition for "what can I charge?" in new contexts
