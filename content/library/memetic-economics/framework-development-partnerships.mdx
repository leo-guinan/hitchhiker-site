## What It Is

Framework Development Partnerships are structured collaborations where two or more parties co-develop or co-validate frameworks. The partnership isn't just division of laborâ€”it's validation infrastructure. When someone else builds on your framework, tests it, or extends it, they're not just "using" it; they're increasing its legitimacy and robustness. Partnerships turn frameworks into living infrastructure.

## What It Produces

- Legitimacy through collaboration (others stake reputation on your work)
- Robustness through application (frameworks get tested in new contexts)
- Distribution through partners (each partner has their own audience)
- Compound growth (your framework + their context = new value)

## When to Use It

- You have a framework that could be applied in other domains
- You want validation beyond "I said so" (partners as proof)
- You're building something that benefits from multiple perspectives
- You want to scale impact without scaling yourself (partners extend the work)

## How to Apply It

1. **Identify complementary partners**: Who has context you don't? Who could apply your framework in their domain?
2. **Define the partnership structure**: Co-authored piece? Joint workshop? Licensed application? Clear roles and credit.
3. **Make the framework usable**: They need to be able to take it and run. Document, templates, support.
4. **Share upside**: If the partnership creates value, how is it shared? (Revenue, attribution, co-branding.)
5. **Validate publicly**: Co-created outputs (posts, workshops, case studies) that show the framework in action.
6. **Iterate together**: Partnerships should improve the framework. Feedback loop from partners to core.

## Where It Breaks

- When partners don't have real skin in the game (performative partnership)
- When the framework is too vague to apply (partners can't operationalize)
- When credit or value sharing is unclear (conflict later)
- When you need control and partnership requires letting go

## Who It's For

Framework builders, researchers, consultants building intellectual property with others

## Observable Outcomes

- Frameworks that show up in others' work (with attribution)
- Case studies and applications you didn't have to create alone
- Stronger legitimacy (multiple parties endorse and use)
- Extended reach (partners' audiences discover your work)
