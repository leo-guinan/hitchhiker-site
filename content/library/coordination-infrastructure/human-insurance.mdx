## What It Is

Human Insurance is the principle that people need protection—structural, not just cultural—to tell the truth. Without it, they'll optimize for safety over accuracy. Whistleblowers get destroyed, critics get sidelined, and the system loses reality contact. Human Insurance is the set of design choices that make it possible to speak up without being destroyed.

## What It Produces

- Conditions where truth-telling is possible
- Reduced self-censorship (people don't have to choose between honesty and survival)
- Earlier detection of problems (people report instead of hiding)
- Alignment between individual incentive and collective need for truth

## When to Use It

- Designing organizations or communities where feedback is critical
- When you've seen good people stay silent (and then things blew up)
- When building systems that depend on bad news traveling fast
- When you want to resist capture (insiders need to be able to say "this is wrong")

## How to Apply It

1. **Identify the risks**: What happens to someone who tells an inconvenient truth? (Reputation, role, relationship, literal safety.)
2. **Design protection**: Anonymous channels, non-retaliation policies, exit options, legal or structural backing. Make it real, not symbolic.
3. **Verify protection**: Do people actually use the channels? If not, why not? (Often the "protection" is performative.)
4. **Connect to LoveOps**: Protection without care is cold. Care without protection is naive. Both needed.
5. **Connect to TrustOps**: Protection enables truth; verification ensures it's not abused. Balance both.
6. **Document and enforce**: What happens when someone retaliates against a truth-teller? If the answer is vague, you don't have Human Insurance.

## Where It Breaks

- When protection is announced but not enforced (people learn the hard way)
- When the only "protection" is anonymity (no accountability, easy to game)
- When powerful people are exempt (they can retaliate without consequence)
- When the system rewards loyalty over truth (insurance doesn't matter if the culture overrides it)

## Who It's For

Org designers, community stewards, anyone building systems where truth-telling must survive power asymmetries

## Observable Outcomes

- People raise concerns instead of quitting quietly
- Bad news travels faster (fewer surprises)
- Lower cost of truth (fewer people destroyed for speaking)
- Systems that can self-correct before crisis
